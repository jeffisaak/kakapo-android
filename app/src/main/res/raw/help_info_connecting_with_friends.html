Overview

Connecting with friends on Kakapo involves exchanging IDs. This can happen through email, instant messaging, scanning a QR code, or if you're feeling particularly sadistic, typing it out manually.

Sharing your ID with others

To share your ID with others, navigate to the "Me" screen and tap the share button. You can share your ID through showing a QR code on the screen, or by sending your ID via an app you have installed on your phone (such as email or an instant messaging app).

Adding your friends

To add a friend to Kakapo, navigate to the "Friends" screen and tap the add button, then tap the "Add friend from QR code" button (if you want to scan a friend's ID with your camera), the "Add friend from clipboard" button (if you've copied your friends ID to the clipboard), or the "Add friend manually" button if you want to type out your friends ID.

You will then be prompted to name your friend. This can be whatever you choose, and needn't be your friends real name. It is only present on your device.

Strangers

It is possible for someone to share things with you if they have your ID and you do not have theirs. The items in your news feed will be marked as being from someone you do not have in your friends list, and instead of their name you will see their ID. You can add this person as a friend by tapping the add friend button on the item.

If you'd rather not see news items from strangers, you may use the news filter to hide items from strangers.

Kakapo accounts

Your Kakapo account data is stored on your device, and encrypted and backed up to the Kakapo servers. Any time you make changes to your Kakapo account (such as adding friends or groups), your account data is encrypted and uploaded to the Kakapo server so that it can be synchronized to any other devices that you want to use your Kakapo account on.

Then, when you launch Kakapo, it checks to see if the account data has been updated by one of your other devices, and synchronizes the account on your device with the updated account info on the server.

When this happens, your account data is encrypted on your device using your password before it is uploaded to the Kakapo server. When account data is uploaded, it is decrypted on your device. This means that your devices can stay in sync while we continue to protect your privacy.

Syncing and backups

Please read the help pages on syncing your account to another device and backing up your account.

A warning

If you do not have a backup of your account and you must reinstall Kakapo for any reason, you will lose access to your account, and there is nothing we can do about it.
